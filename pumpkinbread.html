Pumpkin Bread

Recipe Link

Ingredients

  • 1 3⁄4 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsps ground cinnamon
  • 1⁄4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1⁄4 tsp ground cloves
  • 1⁄4 tsp ground ginger
  • 3⁄4 tsp salt
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 1⁄2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3⁄4 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 1⁄2 cups pumpkin puree
  • 1⁄2 cup vegetable oil, canola oil, or melted coconut oil
  • 1⁄4 cup orange juice or milk
  • 2⁄3 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Preheat oven to 350°. Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan.

In a large bowl, whisk the flour (1 3⁄4 cups), baking soda (1 tsp), cinnamon (2 tsp), nutmeg (1⁄4 tsp), cloves (1⁄4 tsp), ginger (1⁄4 tsp), and salt (3⁄4 tsp) together until combined. Set aside.

In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs (2), granulated sugar (1⁄2 cup), and brown sugar (3⁄4 cup) together until combined. Whisk in the pumpkin (1 1⁄2 cups), oil (1⁄2), and orange juice (1⁄4 cup). Pour these wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and gently mix together using a rubber spatula or a wooden spoon. There will be a few lumps. Do not over-mix. Gently fold in the chocolate chips (2⁄3 cup), if including.

Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan. Bake for 60–65 minutes, making sure to loosely cover the bread with aluminum foil halfway through to prevent the top from getting too brown. The bread is done when a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean with only a few small moist crumbs. This may be before or after 60–65 minutes depending on your oven, so begin checking every 5 minutes around the 55-minute mark.

Allow the bread to cool completely in the pan on a wire rack before removing and slicing.
