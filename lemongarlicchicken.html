Lemon Garlic Chicken

Recipe Link

Chicken

  • 2 large boneless skinless chicken breasts
  • 1 ½ tsp lemon pepper seasoning
  • 3 TBS flour
  • 1-2 TBS olive oil

Sauce

  • ½ cup dry white wine (or chicken broth)
  • 6-8 garlic cloves (peeled and smashed)
  • 3 TBS butter
  • 3 TBS flour
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 chicken bouillon cube
  • ¼ cup heavy cream
  • ⅓ cup parmesan cheese
  • 3-4 TBS lemon juice

Seasoning

  • ¾ tsp EACH: parsley oregano, basil, mustard powder
  • ½ tsp onion powder

For Serving

  • Lemon wedges
  • Parsley
  • See notes for an optional crunchy panko topping

For the sauce: In a large measuring cup with a spout, combine the chicken broth (2 cups), bouillon cube, heavy cream (¼ cup), and seasonings (½ tsp onion powder & ¾ tsp EACH: parsley oregano, basil, mustard powder). Set aside.

Measure out remaining ingredients before beginning.

For the chicken: Slice the chicken in half lengthwise to create 2-3 thinner slices. Cover with saran wrap and use the textured side of a meat mallet to pound it to ½ inch thick.

Season each side with lemon pepper seasoning. Sprinkle with flour and rub it over the surface of each side. (The flour gives it a little texture and prevents it from sticking to the skillet. It also thickens the sauce a little more at the end.)

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the chicken in batches for 4-5 minutes per side, until a golden crust has developed. Set aside.

Make the sauce: Turn the heat off. Add the chicken broth (½ cup) and then set the heat to medium. Add the (6-8) smashed garlic cloves. Use a silicone spatula to clean the bottom and sides of the skillet, the “fond” will add more flavor to the sauce. Bring to a gentle bubble and reduce by half, about 3 minutes.

Add the butter (3 TBS) and flour (3 TBS) and stir to combine. Cook for 1-2 minutes to cook off the raw flour taste and develop flavor.

Add the chicken broth mixture in small splashes, stirring continuously. (Doing this slowly keeps the sauce thick and prevents the roux from breaking.)

Bring the sauce to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Simmer/reduce for 5 minutes.

Reduce heat to low. Gradually stir in the Parmesan cheese (⅓ cup) until combined, then add the lemon juice (3-4 TBS). (Doing this over no heat ensures that the acidity from the lemon juice doesn’t curdle the cream.)

Add the chicken back and spoon the sauce on top. Heat through for 5 minutes or until the sauce has reached your desired thickness.

Add crunchy panko topping (if using, see notes), and garnish with lemon wedges and parsley. Serve!

Crunchy Panko Topping (Optional)

  • ⅓ cup Panko breakcrumbs
  • 1 TBS salted butter
  • 1⁄8 tsp garlic powder

Instructions: Toast the Panko breadcrumbs and garlic powder in melted butter in a skillet over medium heat until brown, about 3 minutes. Remove and set aside. Sprinkle over the chicken just before serving for a delicious crunch!
